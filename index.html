Icing Transition Zone

Pilots watch 5°C closely. The AW109 can collect ice in "Visible Moisture" (rain/clouds) even above freezing because air speeds over the rotors drop pressure and temperature. Ice = Weight + Vibration.

Power Margin (Cat A)

Think of this as the "Safety Parachute." It’s the percentage of power we have left to climb if one engine dies. If this is < 5%, we have zero room for error during takeoff from a scene.

Ceiling vs Visibility

Ceiling is your "Roof" (clouds above). Visibility is your "Sight" (distance ahead). You need both to reduce CFIT risk. If either fail, the mission should be reviewed against operational policy.

Weight Dynamics

As weight increases, more rotor/engine performance is required. On hot days, air is thinner and available margin can narrow.
